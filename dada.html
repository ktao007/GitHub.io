Form follows visual-Product design for Maison Dada

Maison Dada is a French contemporary furniture brand created by Dariel Studio in Shanghai at 2015. At the beginning when this brand just born, I was the intern to help two major designers Thomas Dariel and Paloma Bouteleux to execute the concepts to visible design.
All final product images' copywrite belong to Masion Dada.





Masion Dada foucs on contemporary design, contemporary art and a contemporary way of thinking. The first collection take a lot inspiration from art and visual reference. The furniture itself telling story via visual language.





During the work, I create 3D models to test the struture and proportions.


The collection of candle stick. Its components could be resembled in many different ways.

The inspiration is from the traditional candle stick, but we redesign the form to modernised it.


Sonia is a dining table that pays homage to the famous French painter Sonia Delaunay, known for her use of strong colours and geometric shapes. Inspired by her oil paintings and as a subtle reflection of abstract art, this piece of furniture breaks away from the traditionalism of ‘dining tables.’


Supported by two cylinder cone legs, this colourful and playful tabletop swirls with bright colours painted on three blended circles. The form follow the visual works.


Fying lamp 2D section view, the structure behind the visual is essential to be well designed.


It has the structure of a regular table lamp re-imagined into a whimsical suspended form. Freed from the need to be placed onto something, one can spontaneously choose its position, whether hung on its own or highlighting a piece of furniture.


A sense of
nonsense

Fulfilling the function of a regular floor lamp, the giant flying lamp is also designed as a fantastical suspended object. It features an oversized lampshade that gives an almost human scale feeling. This levitating character attracts all attention once displayed in a room.


The make up table features a very fine box where one can store their jewellery, as well as two mirrors – a round and extra-large one. I try different combination to find the best solution for functinality.

Ode to
femininity

With dainty lines and a subtle colour combination of soft pastels and pink-brushed metal, this make up table is an ode to femininity. The soft green pastels color that I chose became a main color among the whole collection.


The box table is a nod to the Chinese traditional dining table. As a “furniture in motion”, this coffee table is a visual invitation of movement. The surfaces possess different patterns, colours and textures.


Modling for tripod mirror.


I try many different texture and color to find the eseence for thsi piece. To capsure a feeling of elegence via form and visual language.
